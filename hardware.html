Reasons Why Knowing Hardware Is Important!

Mathematics, Physics, and Computer Hardware knowlege is key to majoring in computor engineering which is a great area of work in the Bay Area. Each computer part plays an essential role in how the computer operates and functions.

The Parts!!!

The Central Processing Unit (CPU)

Just as the nucleus is the brain of the cell, the CPU is the brain of the computer. The CPU is located inside of the motherboard

The Motherboard (Me Mums Board)

Just as the CPU is the brain, the motherboard is the skull. Its primary purpose is to hold all the important PC parts. These parts may include the CPU, memory (RAM), and connectors for other devices.

Monitor (Shiny Screen)

When I was a child I used to call the monitor, the "computer." However, this thing I called a computer, is actually just a screen illuminated with light that shows the results of the processes that have been run by the components of the motherboard. So yes, the screen that is flashing before you is a monitor.

Case (The Tower)

The case is the ultimate form of protection for the motherboard and its components. It not only protects everything, but it holds everything together.

Hard Drive

The hard drive is another part located on the motherboard and is charge of holding all the memory needed to store your information.

Modem

The modem is a device that provides internet access to your computer. I may or not be a seperate component than the motherboard.

Case Fan

The case fan is a larger sized fan used inside the case to keep all the components cool.

CPU Fan

The CPU fan is a smaller sized fan used to keep the CPU cool enough to operate.

Keyboard+Mouse

The keyboard and mouse are not essential to the operation of a computer, but they do give the user the ability to be in complete control over basic operations. The consists of varied characters used in the US, and allows the user to write and create. The mouse allows for quick curser movement along the screen.

Zip Drive

The zip drive is a floppy disc storage system that is a seperate component to the motherboard. Although most floppy discs are obsolete, some computers produced contain them. Floppy discs are close to our current CD's, except they have much less space.

CD Rom Drive

The CD Rom Drive is where we place our CD's in. This part usually comes pre-engraved in the case. This part allows us to move media from one computer to another.
